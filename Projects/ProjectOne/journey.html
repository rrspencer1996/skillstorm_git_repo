  • 2003: Operation Enduring Freedom/Iraqi Freedom


    Deployed as a Data Network systems specialist with the Marine Air Support Squadron 3 (MASS-3) in support of Operation Enduring Freedom. MASS 3 attached directly to 1stMarine Division and provided the commander with direct liason to air support as part of the Marine Air Ground Task Force (MAGTF). My primary role was to assist in the planning and deployment of command and control computer systems and to once in country ensure that network and computer systems were operational throughout combat operations.

  • 2005: 1st Korean Deployment


    Deployed to the Korean penninsula in support of excersize Ulchi Focus Lens '05 (UFL). Conducted multinational exercises with Mission Partner nations designed to counter the aggressions of North Korea. Acted as the Electronic Key Manager for the exercise and was directly responsible for maintaining the secure communication keys for the radio and computer systems needed for the unit and subordinate commands.

  • 2010: Marine Recruiter RS Fort Lauderdale!


    Selected to be a Marine Recruiter in the state of Florida. Responsible for conducting school and home visits, conducting interviews and ensuring Marine candidates where ready to take on the role of a United States Marine, both mentally and physically. During my tour of duty I successfully recruited more then sixty candidates into the Marine Corps. Additionally, was selected to manage the Poolee program for the command for the last few months of service before completing my successful tour. As a result of our efforts my recruiting substation one Recruiting substation of the year 3 years running.

  • 2012: Data Chief, MARFORPAC, HI


    Assigned as Lead Engineer (Data Chief) for Marine Forces Pacific. Led a team of over 20 Marines, and Civilian contractors responsible for providing enterprise IT Services to the MarForPac Commanding General and his staff, as well as directly provide strategic command and control support for subordinate units throughout the MarForPac area of responsibility. During my tenure the command successfully upgraded over 60 legacy end of life servers and networking components, greatly improved the stability and survivability of services and modernized its strategic server stack directly impacting Fleet Marine forces throughout the Marine Forces Pacific AOR.

  • 2019: Retirement from the Military


    Retired from the Marine Corps after an successful tour with CTF 76, At CTF 76 I acted as a liason for the Marine Corps to the Navy. After Retirement I continued to work in service to the Navy as a contractor working for General Dynamics Nuclear Weapon Surety (NWS) Program aboard King's Bay Naval Facility in Georgia. I worked alongside a small team of cyber security contractors that created a security program from the ground up and ensured that the systems that provided security to the facilities that housed Nuclear weapons was free from tamper and malicious intent. Our team accomplished alot however, most of the details are classified.

  • 2021: Start up


    Worked at a small start up Managed Service Provider (MSP) called Lannan Technologies. Started as a Information Assurance engineer and eventually promoted to Director of DoD Operations and Lead Engineer for a team of 12 server and network engineers. Our team supported both Government and commercial clients, we directly planned and engineered the corporate network, as well as put into place ITIL based practices to ensure that network and service stability were paramount. During my time there I saw the staff grow from 6 personnel to over 20, and also onboarded new customers for the commercial component of the company.
